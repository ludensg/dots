Humanity is progressively migrating into a digital world. Content and context are now one and the same. In a world where all information, is perfectly preserved and mostly unfiltered without regards for truth or relevance, and in absence of a true reality, it is now up to us to seek something to believe in. To take responsibility of our place in the information age, find power in knowledge, and choose what to pass on to the future.

"Earth may not be forever, but we still have the responsibility to leave what traces of life we can. Building the future and keeping the past alive are one and the same thing." - David

In order to understand what this art piece really is, both as an intervention and as a social sculpture, it is important to first understand what the Wikipedia Eye is not.

The Wikipedia Eye is Not

  1. This Website
  2. Affiliated with Wikipedia in any way, shape, or form (yet): Any connection to the Wikimedia Trademark in this website is intended as an artistic statement in accordance with Section 3.6.3 of the Wikimedia Foundation Trademark Policy, in relation to the nominative use of marks and references to Wikimedia sites for artistic, scientific, literary, political, and other non-commercial uses. The words "Wikipedia Eye" are a single proper noun, derived from the word Wikipedia, but completely independent from the Wikimedia Foundation itself.
  3. A form of political (or any other type of) activism: Any political or public statement in relation to any individual Wikipedia Eye, or to the concept as a whole, is completely incidental.
  4. A method of advertising or self-promotion, or a profit-seeking endeavor: Uses of the Wikipedia Eye concept with lucrative intentions aren't considered as part of its mission.
  5. A disruption of Wikipedia: A breaching experiment, is a social science research method where researchers intentionally break social norms to observe and analyze the reactions of others. This project is not a breaching experiment. Any intervention on Wikipedia that doesn't comply with their terms for notability, verifiability, reliable sources, and no original research, is antithetical to the Wikipedia Eye. So is Wikipedia vandalism. For more information consult Wikipedia's Five Pillars.
  6. Self-sufficient: The Wikipedia Eye concept exists within the relation between the transient marker (as described below) and its connection to sources of documentation. With standards in accordance to Wikipedia's Reliable Source classifications, this website being just a single one of such sources. As each Wikipedia Eye has different information-relationships, each one will have different degrees of effectivity. In addition, theoretically, Wikipedia Eyes that are nearby in association could create a cluster of support, whereby one Wikipedia Eye with a stronger informational foundation (external coverage), helps lower the bar of documentation for the other Eyes in that particular cluster.
  7. Unchanging.

The Wikipedia Eye Is

An invitation to explore your relationship to the consumption, generation, understanding, and witnessing of information, knowledge, and art, based upon a set of core principles to delimit its purpose and approach.

Core Principles

  1. Impermanence: The Wikipedia Eye is a transient marker. A Wikipedia Eye art piece ceases to exist physically after it has fulfilled it's purpose of interacting with the flow of information, as the eye itself is then transduced into information. What's left behind in it's wake (i.e. a painting), is just a trace of it's previous physical container.
  2. Persistence: Persistence in computer science refers to the characteristic of state of a system that outlives (persists more than) the process that created it. In hardware storage, this refers to the ability of data to remain stored and intact even when the storage device is not powered. This means that the data will still be accessible the next time the device is powered on. Common examples of persistent storage devices include hard drives, SSDs, USB drives, and optical discs. In contrast, RAM (Random Access Memory) in computers is a form of non-persistent storage, as its contents are lost when the power is turned off. The Wikipedia Eye concept relies upon a process of transducing physical artifacts of ephemeral importance (i.e. paintings) into persistent (and, if possible, relevant) information.
  3. Connection: In the spirit of the Wikimedia Movement, The Wikipedia Eye celebrates the connection with like-minded indivudals present, past, and future.
  4. Open Access: Just as Wikipedia believes in free access to the sum of all human knowledge, The Wikipedia Eye encourages open dialogue, interpretation, and understanding. And as such, efforts need to be made in order to raise information from a state of unknowningness, up to a level of accessibility.
  5. Action: Even though The Wikipedia Eye isn't a form of activism, it exists in appreciation, and in consequence, of the doing of important things.
