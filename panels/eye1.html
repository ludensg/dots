The Elmyr de Hory Exhibit

Eye 1 - Acrylic on Canvas, 3 x 3 inches. July, 2023.

This Wikipedia Eye records the Elmyr de Hory Exhibit, which was on display for a limited time at the Museum of Fine Arts at Florida State University, starting July 12th, along with peripheral elements spread around the FSU campus and surrounding areas.

This exhibit is pseudepigraphically attributed (and in tribute) to Hungarian-born painter and performance artist Elmyr de Hory. It consists of a set of mixed media pieces, as described below in more detail (in the Pieces section), and has received public media coverage in the FSView & Florida Flambeau Newspaper (both online and in print), as well as in the barstoolfsu Instagram account (a direct affiliate of sports and pop culture blog Barstool Sports). Further details on this can be found in the Media Coverage section below. The whereabouts of the mentioned pieces are currently unknown, with exception of the painting of Piece D.

Exhibition Introduction

As seen in the picture, a text written by Dr. Ludens O'Possum (alleged exhibition curator), introduces the exhibition, and is located at the entrance of the gallery floor:

"As we look towards the future in our neighbouring exhibit, Southern Futurisms, we must also collectively understand the present. I have assembled and curated this little exhibit out of items from my own personal collection. I have long been an admirer of Elmyr, whom I have personally met and remember quite dearly. But, I believe that these pieces have spent enough time in the dark. These pieces are facing, just as the artist (and others) did before them, the possibility of the greatest danger and disservice known to humanity: Domicide.

I hope then that, within these museum's walls, and within the walls of your mind, these five pieces may not only find a place to be understood, but most importantly, a place to be.

In line with the artist's instructions, the titles of the showcased artworks are presented independently and not exhibited alongside their respective pieces.

Here they are, in no particular order: Infinity Goes Up On Trial, I Countain Multitudes, Cinéma Vérité, Everybody Must Get Stoned, [and] You Can't Wake Up If You Don't Fall Asleep."
Got something to say? opossumludens@gmail.com
- Dr. Ludens O'Possum

The Pieces

The Elmyr de Hory exhibit is made up of three pieces, each a composite of three sub-pieces (a total of three pseudo-tryptichs), a number of related peripheral objects (including flyers), and a stand-alone painting. An illustration is provided as a map for the placement of the parts of this exhibit that were displayed inside the museum.

Each piece is presented along with the same information as displayed in their corresponding museum placques. Any additional information is presented in brackets, and any piece from a neighbouring exhibit has been blurred for privacy's sake.
